Overview

Alchemic Soul is a composition by Simone Valente and Enrico Cesarin for string quintet, organ, and electronic tracks, created for QUADRI: Music, Audiovisuals, Performance, held in Trieste in May 2025. The work emerges from the interaction between instrumental writing and electronic sound design, developing a sonic language that moves through orchestral, ambient, and electroacoustic dimensions. Presented within a multidisciplinary format dedicated to original works created and performed by students of the G. Tartini Conservatory, the composition exists in a context where music, visuals, and performance merge into a continuous expressive flow.

Through harmonic tension, layered timbres, and suspended atmospheres, Alchemic Soul builds a sonic journey oscillating between introspection and transformation, translating a deeply personal visual and emotional universe into music.

Process

The composition originated as an attempt to musically translate the expressive details of Alchemic Soul, a painting by Trieste-based artist Consuelo Rodriguez, conceived as a representation of her own inner self. The creative process developed in two complementary stages. First, Simone Valente composed the instrumental writing, associating visual and emotional elements of the painting with specific musical, timbral, and harmonic choices. Afterwards, Enrico Cesarin created the electronic components, conceived as both an extension and a framing element of the acoustic composition. The electronic sections, functioning as the introduction and ending of the piece, gradually intertwine with the ensemble through layers of sine waves that generate suspended chordal atmospheres. Certain frequencies intentionally oscillate between consonance and dissonance, creating subtle tensions that are later resolved by the entrance of the acoustic ensemble. The result is a continuous dialogue between organic material and electronic synthesis, between instability and balance. The waves used in the electronic part were initially derived through a process of visual analysis and mapping of the painting, transforming selected chromatic and compositional elements into sonic data. The resulting material was then processed through SPEAR (Sinusoidal Partial Editing Analysis and Resynthesis), a software tool dedicated to spectral analysis and sinusoidal partial manipulation, used to shape and reconstruct the harmonic textures within the composition.
