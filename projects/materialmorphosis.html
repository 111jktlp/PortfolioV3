Overview

MATERIALMORPHOSIS 2.0 is an interactive audiovisual installation that investigates the dynamic relationship between gesture, material transformation, and perception. Rather than presenting a fixed narrative, the work constructs a responsive system in which the viewer’s bodily actions become the primary driver of change across sensory environments. Interaction is mediated through hand movements and eye closure, which function as triggers for seamless transitions between three distinct visual and sonic states. Each environment represents a different phase of material behavior and perceptual intensity, designed to progressively shift the user’s experience of space and matter. The first scene serves as an introductory threshold: a suspended, interactive particulate field resembling a floating dust cloud. This environment establishes a sense of emergence and instability, where minimal gestures influence subtle dispersions and reconfigurations of particles, introducing the logic of the system.

The subsequent environments shift toward more elemental conditions. One is inspired by water and the other represents magma. Together, these three stages form a progressive exploration of matter in transition, where interaction becomes a catalyst for perceptual and material metamorphosis.

Process

This project originated as a collaborative artistic experiment developed within an academic context, with the aim of being presented at science and cultural events such as Trieste Next and Caleidoscienze. The installation is conceived as an interactive audiovisual environment composed of a screen, sound system, and webcam, through which the audience directly engages with the system. Using MediaPipe technology, the user’s movements and gestures are detected and interpreted in real time, creating an immersive experience based on bodily interaction. The sound component is developed in Max, which is used for data reception, processing, and dynamic sound environment management. At the same time, TouchDesigner, connected via the OSC protocol, handles the visual and graphic processing of the installation, establishing a continuous dialogue between sound, image, and movement. The result is an interactive system that transforms the visitor’s presence and gestures into expressive elements, giving rise to a dynamic and participatory audiovisual experience.
